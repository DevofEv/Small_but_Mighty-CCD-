Design Principles for Success

How to make civic engagement work for everyone

Intentional Inclusion

Proactively remove barriers through multilingual materials, low voting ages, no citizenship requirements, stipends, and targeted outreach to marginalized communities.

Radical Transparency

Make all processes open and understandable. Publish selection methods, provide public reports, and maintain interactive project maps.

Clear Mandate & Scope

Define purpose, questions, and extent of citizen influence from the start. Manage expectations and ensure recommendations are actionable.

Adequate Resourcing

Commit sufficient financial, human, and technical resources. Fund dedicated staff, expert facilitation, and participant support.

Skilled Facilitation

Use trained, neutral facilitators to guide discussions, ensure respectful dialogue, and help groups reach considered judgments.

Continuous Learning

Evaluate processes and outcomes regularly. Use findings to improve future engagement and demonstrate ongoing commitment to quality.

Democracy is a Design Problem

When we design civic engagement with intentionprioritizing inclusion, transparency, and genuine power-sharingwe unlock the collective wisdom of our communities.

The power to build a better future resides in our communities, waiting to be unlocked by the simple, yet profound, act of engaging citizens as true partners in governance.
